This website is a Wikipedia page about Donald Trump, the 45th president of the United States. It details his life, business career, and political career, highlighting his controversial comments and actions.

Early Life and Business Career:

Born in Queens, New York City in 1946, Trump is the fourth child of Fred Trump and Mary Anne MacLeod Trump.
He attended Fordham University and the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, graduating with a Bachelor of Science in Economics.
Trump took over his family's real estate business in 1971, renaming it The Trump Organization and focusing on constructing and renovating skyscrapers, hotels, casinos, and golf courses.
Despite experiencing several business failures in the late 1990s, he launched side ventures, including reality television series "The Apprentice."
He has been involved in over 4,000 legal actions, including six business bankruptcies.
Political Career:

Trump registered as a Republican in 1987 and ran for president in 2000, 2012, and 2016.
He won the 2016 presidential election, becoming the first president without prior military or government service.
His campaign positions were populist, protectionist, and nationalist.
His presidency was marked by numerous protests and a significant rise in partisan division.

He rolled back environmental regulations and signed the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017.

He initiated a trade war with China and withdrew the U.S. from the Trans-Pacific Partnership, Paris Agreement, and Iran Nuclear Deal.
He met with North Korean leader Kim Jong Un three times, but made no progress on denuclearization.
He reacted slowly to the COVID-19 pandemic.
Post-Presidency:

Trump lost the 2020 presidential election to Democratic candidate Joe Biden and refused to concede.
He made false claims of widespread electoral fraud and attempted to overturn the results.
He is the only U.S. president to have been impeached twice.
He continues to dominate the Republican party and is currently running for president in the 2024 election.
He has been accused of mishandling classified documents, interfering in the 2020 election, and committing financial fraud.
He has been convicted of falsifying business records related to a hush money payment to porn star Stormy Daniels, making him the first U.S. president to be convicted of a felony.
Trump's 2024 Presidential Election:

In November 2024, Trump was elected for a second non-consecutive term as president, defeating incumbent vice president Kamala Harris.
He became the second U.S. president elected to serve non-consecutive terms.
His campaign was marked by increasing violent and authoritarian statements.
He has also threatened to weaponize the FBI and the Justice Department against his political opponents and to use the military against them.
His embrace of far-right extremism and harsh rhetoric have been described by historians as unprecedented in American politics.
Concerns about his age and health also emerged during the campaign.
Trump's ear was grazed by a bullet in an assassination attempt at a campaign rally in Pennsylvania.
He was formally nominated as their presidential candidate at the 2024 Republican National Convention, with Senator JD Vance as his running mate.
